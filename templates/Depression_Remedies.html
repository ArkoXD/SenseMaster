Mild Depression

Mild depression is characterized by mild symptoms that don't significantly interfere with daily life.

What you can do to make yourself feel better.

  1. Exercise-Regular exercise can boost your mood if you have depression, and it's especially useful for people with mild to moderate depression.
    • do strengthening activities that work all the major muscle groups (legs, hips, back, abdomen, chest, shoulders and arms) on at least 2 days a week
    • do at least 150 minutes of moderate intensity activity a week or 75 minutes of vigorous intensity activity a week
    • spread exercise evenly over 4 to 5 days a week, or every day
    • reduce time spent sitting or lying down and break up long periods of not moving with some activity
  2. Yoga: Yoga can be a beneficial complementary approach for mild depression. Regular yoga practice, including gentle poses, deep breathing, and meditation, may help reduce stress, improve mood, and increase overall well-being by promoting relaxation and mindfulness.
  3. Meditation: Like yoga, meditation is an excellent way for you to learn how to relax your mind and efficiently refocus it.

Moderate Depression

Depressed mood along with the presence of somatic symptoms was an indicator of moderate depression. People with moderate depression are more likely to experience primary symptoms of low mood, sleep difficulties, weight or appetite changes, and increased/slowed psychomotor activity.

What you can do to make yourself feel better:

  1. Reading books-
    • FEELING GOOD: THE NEW MOOD THERAPY BY DAVID BURNS.
    • SELF-COMPASSION: THE PROVEN POWER OF BEING KIND TO YOURSELF BY KRISTIN NEFF
